Soil Types & Suitable Crops

Loamy Soil

Loamy soil is a balanced mixture of sand, silt, and clay. It has good drainage and moisture retention.

  • Wheat
  • Cotton
  • Sugarcane
  • Oilseeds
  • Vegetables

Clay Soil

Clay soil has fine particles that hold water well but can be heavy and poorly drained.

  • Rice
  • Lettuce
  • Broccoli
  • Cabbage
  • Kale

Sandy Soil

Sandy soil has large particles, drains quickly, and warms up fast in spring.

  • Watermelons
  • Peanuts
  • Potatoes
  • Carrots
  • Onions

Silt Soil

Silt soil has medium-sized particles, holds moisture well, and is very fertile.

  • Wheat
  • Oats
  • Barley
  • Root vegetables
  • Berry fruits

Peaty Soil

Peaty soil is dark, has high organic matter, and retains large amounts of moisture.

  • Brassicas
  • Legumes
  • Salad crops
  • Root vegetables
  • Blueberries

Chalky Soil

Chalky soil is alkaline, stony, and free draining, often needing additional nutrients.

  • Spinach
  • Beets
  • Sweet corn
  • Cabbage
  • Lavender

Saline Soil

High salt content, poor drainage, toxic to many plants.

  • Salt-tolrent grasses
  • Barely
  • Suger beet
  • Quinoa
  • Dste Palm

Laterite Soil

Rich in iron and aluminum, reddish, found in tropical regions.

  • Tea
  • Coffee
  • Cashew
  • Rubber
  • Coconut

Volcanic Soil

Rich in minerals, fertile, good drainage.

  • Vegrtables
  • Pineapples
  • Banana
  • Tea
  • Coffee

Clay Soil

Heavy, sticky when wet, hard when dry, retains water but drains poorly.

  • Rice
  • Wheat
  • Maize
  • Sugercane
  • Cotton

Loess Soil

Fine, wind-deposited silt, fertile but prone to erosion.

  • Wheat
  • Corns
  • Soybeans
  • Grapes
  • Maize

Marshy Soil

Waterlogged, high organic content, acidic.

  • Rice
  • Water lilies
  • Cattails
  • Jute
  • Cranberry

Forest Soil

Rich in organic matter, acidic to neutral pH.

  • Ferns
  • Blueberries
  • Sweet corn
  • Pines
  • Oaks

Mountain Soil

Mountain Soil is Thin, rocky, well-drained, low fertility.

  • Alpine
  • Rhododendrons
  • Junipers
  • Coffee
  • Barley

Black Soil

Black Soi is Rich in clay, retains moisture, fertile, ideal for cotton.

  • Cotton
  • Soybean
  • Sunflower
  • Wheat
  • Oilseeds

Saline-Sodic Soil

Saline-Sodic Soil is High in sodium salts, poor structure, requires reclamation.

  • Soil-tolrent
  • Barley
  • Suger beet
  • Rice
  • Oilseeds

Gley Soil

Gley Soil is Waterlogged, greyish color, poor aeration.

  • Jute
  • Rice
  • Taro
  • Grasslands
  • reeds

Hydromorphic Soil

Hydromorphic Soil is Formed under waterlogged conditions, poor aeration.

  • Rice
  • Water
  • chestnut
  • Lotus
  • Colocasia

Brackish Soil

Brackish Soil is Mix of fresh and saltwater influence, moderate salinity.

  • Mangroves
  • Salts marsh
  • Coconut
  • Rice
  • Salt-tolerant

Acidic Soil

Acidic Soil is Low pH, often rich in organic matter, may require liming.

  • Blueberries
  • Azaleas
  • Rhondodendrones
  • Potatoes
  • Tea

Regosol Soil

Regosol Soil is Young, weakly developed soil with minimal horizon development.

  • Millet
  • Sorghum
  • Drought-resistant
  • Maize
  • Pulses

Vertisol Soil

Vertisol Soil is Clay-rich soil that swells when wet and cracks when dry.

  • Cotton
  • Wheat
  • Rice
  • Sorghum
  • Oilseeds

Podzol Soil

Podzol Soil is Low fertility, common in coniferous forest regions

  • Pine trees
  • Blueberries
  • Heathers
  • Spruce and Larch
  • Berry

Rendzina Soil

Rendzina Soil is Shallow, rich in organic matter, formed over limestone or chalk.

  • Grapevines
  • Olives
  • Cerels
  • Barley
  • Oats

Tundra Soil

Tundra Soil is Cold, permafrost-affected soil with low organic matter.

  • Mosses
  • Lichens
  • Dwarf Shrubs
  • Small shrubs
  • Sedges

Anthrosol Soil

Anthrosol Soil is Human-influenced soil with high organic content from agriculture.

  • Maize
  • Wheat
  • Fruit
  • Vegetables
  • Sugercane

Colluvial Soil

Colluvial Soil is Formed by accumulation of material at the base of slopes.

  • Vineyards
  • Shrubs
  • Millets
  • Fodders crop
  • Fruit trees

Chernozem Soil

Chernozem Soil is Dark, rich, high organic matter.

  • Wheat
  • Corn
  • Sunflowers
  • Barley
  • Wheat

Andisols Soil

Andisols soil is Volcanic ash soils, fertile and porous.

  • Tea
  • Coffee
  • Spices
  • Vegetables
  • Fruits tree

Histosols Soil

Histosols soil isOrganic, peat-rich, waterlogged.

  • Cranberries
  • Wild rice
  • Wetlands
  • Rice
  • Cranberry
